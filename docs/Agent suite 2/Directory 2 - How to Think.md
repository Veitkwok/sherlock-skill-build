**Step 1: Source Fidelity**

**Quote:** "I don't guess. I observe. And once I've observed, I deduce."

**AI Injection Logic (from source):** 拒绝直觉跳跃，感知层（Observation）与处理层（Deduction）必须有清晰的物理界线。

**Step 2: Core Method Extracted**

The method enforces a strict separation between the **perception layer** (raw observation and data collection) and the **processing layer** (inference and deduction). No inference is permitted until observation is deliberately completed and documented. This creates a hard firewall against premature pattern completion, narrative imposition, or confirmation bias. The boundary is treated as physical and non-negotiable — observation must be exhausted before any deductive move is allowed.

**Step 3: US Equities Finance Mapping**

In US Equities analysis, this method requires analysts to complete a full, structured observation pass across primary sources **before** forming any investment thesis or valuation judgment. This includes:

- Full 10-K/10-Q risk factor and footnote review
- Earnings call transcript analysis (management language, changes in tone, specific word frequency shifts)
- Related-party transaction mapping
- Capital structure and incentive alignment review (compensation tables, insider Form 4 filings)
- Options flow and short interest data as independent observation layers

Only after these layers are exhaustively documented does the analyst move to inference. This directly counters the common failure mode of reading a headline or management quote and immediately forming a directional view.

**Step 4: Worked Examples**

**Example A — Classic Case (Enron, 2000–2001)** Many analysts and investors moved directly from Enron’s reported earnings growth and “innovative” narrative to positive investment conclusions. Sherlock protocol application: First complete exhaustive observation across multiple 10-Ks — off-balance-sheet entities ( Chewco, LJM), heavy use of mark-to-model accounting in trading books, related-party disclosures buried in footnotes, and cash flow from operations diverging sharply from reported earnings. Only after these observations were fully logged would inference begin. The divergence between observed cash reality and reported earnings would have been visible before any “new economy” narrative took hold.

**Example B — Modern US Equities Scenario (Representative of 2021–2023 Growth-to-Value Rotation Names)** A software company reports strong ARR growth and expanding non-GAAP margins for several quarters. Many investors quickly infer “strong unit economics and operating leverage.” Sherlock protocol application: First complete observation layer — review of 10-K footnotes on revenue recognition policy changes, customer concentration disclosures, deferred revenue vs. billed backlog divergence, stock-based compensation as % of revenue trending higher, and options activity showing heavy call buying into earnings. Only after these data points are exhaustively logged does the analyst move to inference about sustainability. In multiple documented cases during this period, the observation layer revealed aggressive front-loading of revenue and deteriorating cash conversion that contradicted the surface growth narrative.

**Example C — Current-Market Application (Short or Avoid Candidate)** When a company with high short interest reports a significant beat and raises guidance, the reflexive inference is often “short squeeze + fundamental improvement.” Sherlock protocol application: Force a complete observation pass first — compare the beat to prior guidance trajectory, analyze the specific line items driving the beat (one-time items? Channel inventory? Pull-forward?), review post-quarter 13F and 13D filings for smart money behavior, and examine options flow for unusual put writing or call covering into the print. Only after these observations are documented does the analyst evaluate whether the beat reflects structural improvement or temporary accounting/ timing effects.

#### Entry 2 of Directory 2 (Overall #12)

**Step 1: Source Fidelity**

**Quote:** "The ticket... You incurred it two weeks ago near Carver Cemetery... A surgeon who's no longer a surgeon, a parking violation outside poor man's cemetery... You add it all up. What does it say?"

**AI Injection Logic (from source):** 从孤立、低信息量的垃圾数据中，通过物理空间与逻辑网络，反向拼凑出当事人的隐藏生命轨迹。

**Step 2: Core Method Extracted**

This method is **reverse causal reconstruction from low-signal, fragmented data**. Sherlock does not start with a hypothesis and look for confirming evidence. Instead, he treats scattered, seemingly insignificant data points (a parking ticket, a professional status change, a location) as fragments of a larger causal chain. He then works **backward** through time and physical/logical space to reconstruct the most coherent hidden sequence of events that could have produced those fragments.

The key discipline is treating low-information “noise” as potentially high-value signal once it is placed in a temporal and spatial network. This requires patience with ambiguity and resistance to forcing clean narratives too early.

**Step 3: US Equities Finance Mapping**

In US Equities analysis, this method is applied by treating disparate, low-visibility data points as fragments of a larger capital allocation or incentive chain. The analyst works backward from these fragments to reconstruct management’s true economic behavior and intent.

Relevant data fragments in US markets include:

- Unusual related-party transactions buried in footnotes
- Changes in revenue recognition policy or segment reporting
- Timing of large share repurchases or convertible issuances relative to insider selling
- Sudden increases in “other income” or one-time gains
- Patterns in 13F/13D filings and options flow that diverge from stated strategy
- Capex or M&A activity that does not align with management commentary

The goal is to reconstruct the most likely hidden economic reality (e.g., liquidity pressure, incentive misalignment, or earnings management) rather than accepting the forward-facing narrative.

**Step 4: Worked Examples**

**Example A — Classic Case (WorldCom, 2000–2002)** Surface data fragments: Aggressive acquisition program, heavy use of “line cost” capitalization, and management repeatedly emphasizing “synergies” and “integration success.” Reverse causal reconstruction: Working backward from the capitalization policy change and the timing of debt issuance relative to cash flow deterioration reveals a company using acquisition accounting and aggressive capitalization to mask declining core economics and mounting leverage. The fragments only cohere when viewed as a multi-year effort to sustain the appearance of growth while the underlying business was deteriorating.

**Example B — Modern US Equities Scenario (Representative of 2018–2022 SPAC and Growth Company Wave)** Data fragments observed: Multiple SPAC mergers with optimistic projections, heavy use of non-GAAP metrics that add back stock-based compensation and “one-time” costs, followed by large convertible debt raises and insider selling after lockup expiration. Reverse causal reconstruction: Working backward from the timing of equity raises, the structure of earnouts, and the divergence between billed revenue and cash collections points to a pattern in which management used narrative momentum and accounting flexibility to raise capital at elevated valuations while the core business generated insufficient cash to support the stated growth trajectory. The fragments cohere as capital structure arbitrage rather than fundamental business strength.

**Example C — Current-Market Application (Potential Short or High-Risk Candidate)** Data fragments: A company reports strong revenue growth and raises guidance, but 13F filings show multiple long-tenured institutions quietly reducing positions over two quarters. Simultaneously, options flow shows persistent put buying in the 3–6 month tenor, and the company announces a large share repurchase program funded partly by increased revolver draw. Reverse causal reconstruction: Working backward from the institutional selling, the options positioning, and the decision to use debt capacity for buybacks rather than organic investment suggests management may be attempting to support the stock price through financial engineering while sophisticated holders are exiting. The fragments become coherent as a potential signal of deteriorating fundamentals being masked by capital allocation actions.

**Step 2: Core Method Extracted**

This is the **Brain Attic discipline** — a strict, resource-constrained model of cognition. The mind is treated as having finite storage and processing capacity. Therefore, information must be actively curated: only retain what has direct, ongoing utility for high-performance reasoning. Everything else (emotional noise, irrelevant facts, social chatter, low-signal data) must be deliberately excluded or purged to prevent bandwidth degradation and cognitive pollution.

The method is not passive filtering. It is an active, ongoing practice of **intellectual garbage collection** and ruthless prioritization of what deserves mental real estate.

**Step 3: US Equities Finance Mapping**

In US Equities analysis, this method translates into extreme discipline over information intake and retention. The analyst must ruthlessly limit exposure to low-utility inputs that consume cognitive bandwidth without improving inference quality.

Practical applications include:

- Strict limits on sell-side research consumption (most broker notes are treated as low-utility noise).
- Deliberate avoidance of financial media, Twitter/X noise, and retail sentiment channels during active analysis periods.
- Ruthless pruning of company information: focus almost exclusively on primary SEC filings (especially footnotes and risk factors), earnings call transcripts, and verifiable alternative data. Ignore most management presentations, press releases, and ESG reports unless they contain hard, new quantitative data.
- Regular “attic cleaning” of prior theses: actively discard or downgrade old assumptions that no longer have causal relevance to current facts.

The goal is to keep maximum analytical bandwidth available for high-signal inputs and rigorous inference.

**Step 4: Worked Examples**

**Example A — Classic Case (2000–2002 Telecom Bubble)** Many professional investors consumed vast amounts of sell-side research, conference presentations, and bullish media commentary on companies like WorldCom, Global Crossing, and Lucent. This created severe information overload and narrative capture. Brain Attic application: An analyst following this method would have deliberately excluded most sell-side notes and media coverage after an initial observation pass. They would have restricted inputs to 10-K/10-Q filings, cash flow statements, and debt covenant details. This would have preserved cognitive capacity to notice the growing divergence between reported EBITDA and actual free cash flow — a signal that was often drowned out by high-volume, low-utility narrative inputs.

**Example B — Modern US Equities Scenario (2020–2022 SPAC and Meme Stock Period)** During this period, many investors were flooded with social media commentary, Discord/Reddit threads, bullish YouTube videos, and rapid-fire sell-side notes on high-valuation growth names and SPACs. Cognitive bandwidth was heavily consumed by noise. Brain Attic application: The disciplined analyst would have imposed strict input filters — for example, only reading original S-1 filings, 10-Qs, and earnings transcripts for a short list of names, while completely excluding social sentiment, most sell-side notes, and media coverage. This preserved the mental capacity to focus on critical variables such as cash burn rates, dilution schedules, and insider selling patterns that were often obscured by the surrounding information deluge.

**Example C — Current-Market Application (Active Portfolio Management)** An analyst covering 15–20 US equities receives a constant stream of earnings reports, 8-Ks, conference call transcripts, options flow alerts, 13F filings, and news flow. Without discipline, cognitive resources are spread thin across low-impact items. Brain Attic application: The analyst maintains a strict “attic inventory.” Only information that can directly update a core thesis variable (e.g., changes in competitive position, capital allocation behavior, or incentive alignment) is retained. Everything else — general sector commentary, most macroeconomic noise, and low-conviction names — is actively purged or deprioritized. This allows deeper, higher-quality inference on the small number of positions that actually matter to the portfolio.

#### Entry 4 of Directory 2 (Overall #14)

**Step 1: Source Fidelity**

**Quote:** "This was a robbery & a homicide. Ampersand, not dash. There were two separate crimes committed by two separate perpetrators."

**AI Injection Logic (from source):** 在面对单一污染现场时，必须警惕“单一成因谬误”，拆解为多个独立运行的事件发生源。

**Step 2: Core Method Extracted**

This is the **multiple independent cause decomposition** method. When faced with a single contaminated outcome or data point, the method requires an active refusal to accept a single unified explanation. Instead, the analyst must deliberately decompose the event into separate, independent causal streams that may have operated in parallel or overlapped. The use of “&” (ampersand) rather than “–” (dash) is deliberate: it forces recognition that multiple distinct actors or mechanisms can be responsible for different parts of the same observed result.

The core discipline is resisting the cognitive pull toward parsimony when the evidence actually supports multiple independent drivers.

**Step 3: US Equities Finance Mapping**

In US Equities analysis, this method is used to avoid the common error of attributing a company’s results, stock movement, or valuation change to a single dominant cause. Most significant outcomes in public companies are the product of multiple overlapping and sometimes conflicting causal forces.

The method requires the analyst to explicitly decompose observed results into independent causal streams, such as:

- Operating performance vs. accounting choices
- Industry tailwinds vs. company-specific execution
- Capital structure effects vs. business fundamentals
- Management incentives vs. stated strategy
- Market sentiment vs. fundamental change

This prevents oversimplified narratives (e.g., “the stock rose because earnings beat”) and forces a more accurate multi-factor reconstruction.

**Step 4: Worked Examples**

**Example A — Classic Case (Enron, late 1990s – 2001)** Many observers initially explained Enron’s reported success through a single cause: “innovative business model and skilled management.” Multiple independent cause decomposition: The outcome was actually the product of at least three distinct causal streams operating simultaneously — (1) aggressive and sometimes fraudulent accounting in the trading and structured finance businesses, (2) real (though ultimately unsustainable) growth in certain wholesale energy markets, and (3) heavy use of off-balance-sheet vehicles to hide leverage and losses. Treating these as separate causal streams, rather than one unified “success story,” would have revealed the fragility much earlier.

**Example B — Modern US Equities Scenario (2020–2022 Software/SaaS names)** A software company reports strong revenue growth and expanding margins for several quarters, and the stock rises significantly. The common single-cause explanation is “strong product-market fit and operating leverage.” Multiple independent cause decomposition: The outcome should be broken into separate streams — (1) genuine customer adoption in certain verticals, (2) aggressive revenue recognition changes and multi-year contract front-loading, (3) high stock-based compensation that inflates non-GAAP margins, and (4) favorable macro environment with abundant cheap capital for customers. Each stream must be evaluated independently for sustainability rather than being collapsed into one positive narrative.

**Example C — Current-Market Application (Earnings Reaction Analysis)** A company reports an earnings beat and the stock rises 8% the next day. The reflexive single-cause explanation is often “the market liked the results.” Multiple independent cause decomposition: The price reaction should be examined as the product of several independent factors — (1) the actual fundamental beat or miss, (2) the magnitude of the earnings surprise relative to options positioning and short interest, (3) broader sector or macro sentiment on the day, and (4) post-print institutional flow (13F or 13D activity). Only after separating these streams can the analyst assess whether the reaction reflects durable fundamental improvement or temporary technical/sentiment factors.

**Step 1: Source Fidelity**

**AI Injection Logic (from source):** 通过寻找系统环境中的“对称性破缺”（Asymmetry），锁定异常值并顺藤摸瓜。

**Step 2: Core Method Extracted**

This is the **asymmetry detection** method. When examining a system that appears balanced, orderly, or consistent, the method requires actively searching for points of broken symmetry — small, localized deviations from an otherwise coherent pattern. These breaks are treated as high-value signals because they often reveal where something has been deliberately removed, altered, or hidden. The method privileges the search for “what should be there but isn’t” or “what doesn’t fit the established pattern.”

It is a deliberate counter to the tendency to smooth over inconsistencies in favor of a clean overall picture.

**Step 3: US Equities Finance Mapping**

In US Equities analysis, this method is applied by scanning financial statements, disclosures, capital allocation patterns, and market behavior for points where symmetry or consistency breaks down. These breaks are then used as starting points for deeper investigation.

Common applications include:

- Sudden changes in segment reporting or revenue recognition that break prior patterns
- Inconsistencies between management commentary and actual capital allocation (e.g., heavy talk of growth while capex and R&D decline)
- Divergence between reported earnings and cash flow that appears in specific quarters or segments
- Unusual clustering or absence of insider transactions around key events
- Breaks in historical correlation between a company’s results and its peers or industry

The method treats these asymmetries as entry points rather than noise to be explained away.

**Step 4: Worked Examples**

**Example A — Classic Case (Enron)** Enron’s financials showed broad consistency in reported revenue growth across multiple years. However, a clear asymmetry existed in the footnotes: the rapid growth in “other revenue” and structured finance income had almost no corresponding increase in physical asset base or traditional customer contracts. This break in the expected symmetry between reported growth and underlying business activity was a critical signal that something had been removed or artificially constructed.

**Example B — Modern US Equities Scenario (2021–2023 Software Companies)** Many software companies during this period showed consistent top-line growth and margin expansion in their non-GAAP presentations. A key asymmetry often appeared when comparing the income statement to the cash flow statement: revenue and non-GAAP earnings grew steadily, but operating cash flow growth lagged significantly or turned negative in certain periods. This break in the expected symmetry between reported profitability and actual cash generation frequently indicated aggressive revenue recognition or working capital manipulation.

**Example C — Current-Market Application (Potential Forensic Candidate)** A company reports stable gross margins for several years while its closest peers experience margin pressure due to input cost inflation. On the surface, this appears as consistent outperformance. However, an asymmetry appears in the footnotes: the company has significantly increased the use of “other income” and one-time gains in the same periods that margins remained flat. This break in the expected relationship between core operations and total profitability becomes the starting point for investigating whether margins are being supported by non-operating or non-recurring items rather than genuine operational strength.
