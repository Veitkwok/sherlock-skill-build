**US Equities Finance Mapping** When a company reports unexpectedly strong results, unusual margin expansion, or timely capital raises, do not default to “good execution” or “fortunate timing.” Instead, demand the specific causal mechanisms and test whether they are repeatable or one-time.

**Worked Examples**

- Strong stock performance following a large convertible issuance — examined for whether the timing was driven by information asymmetry rather than market luck.
