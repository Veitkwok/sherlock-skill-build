**Entry 1 (#21): Review the Givens** **Quote:** "When one is constructing a geometric proof, one must occasionally review the givens." **Core Method:** When stuck or when conclusions conflict with evidence, return to and re-audit the foundational assumptions and data inputs. **US Equities Mapping:** Periodically re-examine core assumptions in a thesis (e.g., normalized margins, competitive position, capital intensity) rather than only updating the model with new data on top of potentially flawed foundations. **Worked Examples:** Re-auditing the assumption that a company’s historical ROIC is sustainable after major changes in competitive intensity or regulation.

**Entry 2 (#22): Provocation as Data Extraction Tool** **Quote:** "I knew that if I started a row... she might very well tell you the truth." **Core Method:** When normal channels fail to produce information, deliberately introduce controlled friction or stress to force hidden variables to surface. **US Equities Mapping:** Use targeted, high-pressure questions in earnings call modeling or scenario analysis to test management claims (e.g., “What would happen to margins if pricing power reversed?”). **Worked Examples:** Stress-testing a high-valuation growth name by forcing explicit assumptions on customer churn and pricing power under competitive pressure.

**Entry 3 (#23): Incompatibility of Behavior with Physical/Financial Reality** **Quote:** "If... the burglar was startled... why was he sitting down?" **Core Method:** Flag behaviors or decisions that are energetically or economically inconsistent with the claimed situation or pressure level. **US Equities Mapping:** Identify capital allocation or disclosure decisions that do not make economic sense under the stated level of stress or opportunity (e.g., large buybacks while burning cash or guiding conservatively). **Worked Examples:** A company raising guidance while simultaneously drawing heavily on its revolver and guiding for higher capex.

**Entry 4 (#24): Role-Behavior Consistency Test** **Quote:** "No doctor would wear her lab coat out of the hospital..." **Core Method:** Test whether observed behavior is consistent with the claimed role or identity. Inconsistencies indicate potential deception or misalignment. **US Equities Mapping:** Evaluate whether management actions (capital allocation, disclosure quality, compensation structure) are consistent with their stated strategy and fiduciary role. **Worked Examples:** Management claiming “long-term thinking” while engaging in heavy financial engineering and large special dividends timed to personal liquidity events.

**Entry 5 (#25): Alternative Data Triangulation** **Quote:** "I don't believe that it was milk in that bottle. I believe it was acetone..." **Core Method:** Use alternative or indirect data to test or falsify official explanations. **US Equities Mapping:** Cross-reference management claims with supplier filings, customer 10-Ks, shipping data, job postings, or credit metrics. **Worked Examples:** A retailer claiming strong same-store sales while suppliers report declining orders and rising returns.

**Entry 6 (#26): Non-Accidental Temporal Correlation** **Quote:** "Two mathematicians... both killed within an hour... Obviously, not a coincidence." **Core Method:** Treat tight temporal clustering of significant events as high-probability evidence of connection rather than coincidence. **US Equities Mapping:** View clustered events (large insider sales + guidance cut + unusual options activity + related-party transaction) as connected until proven otherwise. **Worked Examples:** Multiple large insider sales immediately preceding a major negative revision or restatement announcement.

**Entry 7 (#27): Provocation and Stress-Testing** **Quote:** "I knew that if I started a row... she might very well tell you the truth." **Core Method:** Create controlled adversarial conditions to extract truthful responses or reveal hidden positions. **US Equities Mapping:** Use scenario analysis and pointed modeling questions to force clarity on weak points in a company’s story or financial position. **Worked Examples:** Modeling the impact of a 200–300 bps margin contraction on a high-valuation name to test balance sheet and covenant resilience.

**Entry 8 (#28): Metadata and Indirect Signal Priority** **Quote:** "All West would have been able to determine is the meta-data..." **Core Method:** When direct data is limited or controlled, extract maximum insight from metadata, timing, and indirect signals. **US Equities Mapping:** Heavily weight timing of filings, changes in disclosure language, options positioning, and institutional flow data when primary numbers are ambiguous. **Worked Examples:** Unusual options activity and language softening in the MD&A preceding a major negative event.

**Entry 9 (#29): The Empty Space as Positive Evidence** **Quote:** "I see balance everywhere, except... this one space. Something was here-- what was it?" **Core Method:** Treat deliberate absences or omissions as active information. **US Equities Mapping:** Pay special attention to what is _not_ disclosed, what segments disappear, what metrics stop being reported, or what risks are no longer mentioned. **Worked Examples:** A company stops breaking out a previously highlighted high-growth segment without explanation.

**Entry 10 (#30): Logical Distance of the Principal** **Quote:** "Bradshaw was smarter than your average street thug. He always flew under the radar..." **Core Method:** Higher-intelligence or higher-stakes actors tend to maintain greater distance from direct involvement. Measure causal proximity. **US Equities Mapping:** Evaluate how directly management or insiders are tied to questionable actions versus using layers of intermediaries, structures, or timing. **Worked Examples:** Complex related-party transactions routed through multiple entities as a signal of intentional distancing.
